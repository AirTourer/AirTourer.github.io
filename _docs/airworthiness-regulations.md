Airtourer aircraft operate in several countries, each with their own aviation regulatory framework. This document provides an overview of the key regulations, rules, and resources relevant to the continuing airworthiness of Airtourer aircraft in Australia, New Zealand, and the United States.

Airworthiness and parts support for Airtourer aircraft is provided by the [Airtourer Co-operative](https://www.airtourer.asn.au/).

---

## Australia — Civil Aviation Safety Authority (CASA)

[CASA](https://www.casa.gov.au/) is the Australian Government authority responsible for aviation safety regulation, including aircraft registration, airworthiness certification, and maintenance standards.

### Legislation and Regulations

Australia's aviation maintenance framework currently spans the Civil Aviation Safety Regulations 1998 (CASR), the older Civil Aviation Regulations 1988 (CAR), and Civil Aviation Orders (CAOs). CASA is progressively migrating rules from CAR 1988 to CASR 1998, but for privately operated general aviation aircraft such as the Airtourer, much of the maintenance framework still operates under CAR 1988 and the CAOs.

#### Civil Aviation Safety Regulations 1998 (CASR)

- **[CASR Part 21 — Certification and Airworthiness Requirements for Aircraft and Parts](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/casr/part-21-casr-certification-and-airworthiness-requirements-aircraft-and-parts)**
  Governs type certificates, certificates of airworthiness, supplemental type certificates, and approval of modifications and repairs. The Airtourer holds CASA Type Certificate No. 34-3.

- **[CASR Part 39 — Airworthiness Directives](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/casr/part-39-casr-airworthiness-directives)**
  Sets out the requirements for compliance with Airworthiness Directives (ADs) — mandatory instructions issued when an unsafe condition is identified. The registered operator is legally responsible for compliance with all applicable ADs.

- **[CASR Part 42 — Continuing Airworthiness Requirements](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/casr/part-42-casr-continuing-airworthiness-requirements-aircraft-and-aeronautical-products)**
  Sets overarching requirements for continuing airworthiness management. Currently applies mainly to air transport and aerial work operations, or aircraft over 5,700 kg MTOW. Private GA operators can [elect to manage continuing airworthiness under Part 42](https://www.casa.gov.au/aircraft/airworthiness/continuing-airworthiness-management-organisations/electing-manage-continuing-airworthiness-under-part-42), but most Airtourer operators remain under the CAR 1988/CAO framework.

- **[CASR Part 47 — Registration of Aircraft and Related Matters](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/casr/part-47-casr-registration-aircraft-and-related-matters)**
  Rules for registering, transferring, and cancelling aircraft on the Australian (VH-) register.

- **[CASR Part 66 — Aircraft Engineer Licences and Ratings](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/casr/part-66-casr-continuing-airworthiness-aircraft-engineer-licences-and-ratings)**
  Sets requirements for aircraft maintenance engineer (LAME) licences. Maintenance on the Airtourer must be performed or certified by appropriately licensed persons.

- **[CASR Part 145 — Approved Maintenance Organisations](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/casr/part-145-casr-continuing-airworthiness-approved-maintenance-organisations)**
  Rules for approved maintenance organisations (AMOs) that perform maintenance on aircraft.

#### Civil Aviation Regulations 1988 (CAR)

These are the primary maintenance regulations currently applicable to most privately operated Airtourer aircraft.

- **[CAR Part 4A — Maintenance](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/car/part-4a-car-maintenance)**
  The core maintenance regulations for Australian aircraft not covered by CASR Part 42. Key regulations include:
  - **CAR 42A** — Manufacturer's maintenance schedule
  - **CAR 42B** — CASA maintenance schedule (see Schedule 5)
  - **CAR 42C** — Approved system of maintenance
  - **CAR 42ZC** — Pilot maintenance on Class B aircraft (the Airtourer is a Class B aircraft)

- **[Schedule 5 — CASA Maintenance Schedule](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/car/schedule-5-car-casa-maintenance-schedule)**
  A detailed maintenance schedule that may be used as an alternative to the manufacturer's schedule.

- **[Schedule 8 — Pilot Maintenance Permitted on Class B Aircraft](https://www.casa.gov.au/rules/regulatory-framework/car/schedule-8-car-maintenance-may-be-carried-out-class-b-aircraft-person-entitled-do-so-under-subregulation-42zc4)**
  Lists the simple maintenance tasks a pilot may perform on their own Class B aircraft.

#### Civil Aviation Orders (CAOs)

- **[CAO 100.5 — General Requirements in Respect of Maintenance](https://www.casa.gov.au/search-centre/rules/civil-aviation-orders)**
  The primary CAO for GA maintenance requirements. Applies to all Australian aircraft with a certificate of airworthiness in force, other than those under CASR Part 42. Covers maintenance schedules, maintenance release, defect recording, component life limits, and general maintenance standards.

### Advisory Material

- **[AC 1-04 — Registered Operator Responsibilities for Continuing Airworthiness](https://www.casa.gov.au/registered-operator-responsibilities-continuing-airworthiness)**
  Comprehensive guide covering AD compliance, maintenance controllers, weight and balance, and pilot maintenance. The single most useful document for understanding owner/operator obligations.

- **[CAAP 42.B-01 — CASA Maintenance Schedule](https://www.casa.gov.au/casa-maintenance-schedule)**
  Guidance on using the CASA maintenance schedule (Schedule 5 of CAR).

- **[CAAP 42.ZC-01 — Pilot Maintenance Schedule](https://www.casa.gov.au/part-1-schedule-8-car-1988-pilot-maintenance-schedule-guidance-pilots-and-part-66-licence-holders)**
  Guidance on maintenance tasks that pilots may perform on Class B aircraft.

- **[CAAP 43-01 — Maintenance Release](https://www.casa.gov.au/maintenance-release)**
  Guidance on the maintenance release document.

### Airworthiness Directives

CASA maintains a searchable database of Airworthiness Directives. Airtourer ADs are filed under the series name "Airtourer Cooperative (Victa / A.E.S.L.) Airtourer Series Aeroplanes".

- **[AD Search — Aircraft Below 5,700 kg](https://www.casa.gov.au/aircraft/airworthiness/airworthiness-directives/aircraft-below-5700kg)**
- **[AD Biweekly Listings](https://www.casa.gov.au/search-centre/airworthiness-directives-biweekly-listings)** — newly issued ADs
- **[AD Data Files Download](https://www.casa.gov.au/index.php/aircraft/airworthiness/airworthiness-directives/data-files-all-airworthiness-directives)** — download all AD data

### Type Certificates and Aircraft Register

- **[CASA Type Certificates](https://www.casa.gov.au/aircraft/aircraft-certification/type-certificates)** — includes Airtourer Type Certificate No. 34-3

## New Zealand — Civil Aviation Authority (CAA)

The [New Zealand CAA](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/) is responsible for civil aviation regulation in New Zealand, including aircraft registration, airworthiness certification, and maintenance standards.

The Airtourer Co-operative holds three New Zealand type certificates covering the Airtourer family: Type Certificate A-4 (Airtourer 100 and 115), Type Certificate A-7 (Airtourer 150, Super 150, and T3A), and Type Certificate A-8 (Airtourer T6).

### Civil Aviation Rules

#### Certification and Airworthiness Directives

- **[Part 21 — Certification of Products and Parts](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/rule-part/part-21/)**
  Establishes that aircraft must hold a valid type certificate and airworthiness certificate to operate. Covers issuance of standard, restricted, and special category airworthiness certificates.

- **[Part 39 — Airworthiness Directives](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/rule-part/part-39/)**
  Requires operators to comply with all applicable ADs issued by the Director (Rule 39.51). Allows application for alternative means of compliance (Rule 39.53) and deferral of inspection series by up to 10% for scheduling purposes (Rule 39.55).

#### Maintenance

- **[Part 43 — General Maintenance Rules](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/rule-part/part-43/)**
  Prescribes requirements for maintenance and release to service. Key provisions include:
  - **Rule 43.51** — Who may perform maintenance (licensed AMEs, authorised personnel, or supervised persons; limited pilot maintenance per Appendix A)
  - **Rule 43.69** — Maintenance records requirements
  - **Rules 43.101–43.115** — Release to service (who may certify, requirements, defect recording, duplicate safety inspections of control systems)
  - **Rules 43.151–43.155** — Review of airworthiness (performed by persons with Inspection Authorisation; verifies type certificate conformity, AD compliance, lifed component limits, and general condition)

#### Operating Rules

- **[Part 91 — General Operating and Flight Rules](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/rule-part/part-91/)**
  Contains the core operating and continuing airworthiness obligations for operators:
  - **Rule 91.101** — Aircraft must have a current airworthiness certificate and be in airworthy condition
  - **Rule 91.111** — Documents to be carried (airworthiness certificate, flight manual, technical log, weight and balance)
  - **Rule 91.605** — Maintenance programmes and schedules
  - **Rule 91.615** — Review of airworthiness (within the preceding 12 months, or 24 months for non-hire private operations)
  - **Rules 91.616–91.623** — Maintenance logbooks, records, technical logs, transfer of records, and records retention

#### Registration and Licensing

- **[Part 47 — Aircraft Registration and Marking](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/rule-part/part-47/)**
  Governs ZK- registration requirements.

- **[Part 66 — Aircraft Maintenance Personnel Licensing](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/rule-part/part-66/)**
  Governs licensing of AMEs. Only Part 66 licence holders (or those under their supervision) may perform and certify maintenance.

### Advisory Circulars

The most relevant advisory circulars for Airtourer owner/operators are available from the [Advisory Circulars index](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/).

- **[AC91-14 — Light Aircraft Maintenance Programme — Aeroplanes](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/show/AC91-14/)** — The primary AC for Airtourer maintenance. Covers piston-engine aeroplanes with standard or restricted category airworthiness certificate, MCTOW 2,730 kg or less, not on Part 119 air operations.
- **[AC91-12 — Aircraft Maintenance Programmes](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/show/AC91-12/)** — Guidance on developing and gaining approval of maintenance programmes under Part 91 Subpart G.
- **[AC43-1 — Aircraft Maintenance](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/show/AC43-1/)** — Acceptable means of compliance with Part 43 and Part 91 Subpart G.
- **[AC43-5 — Engine and Propeller Overhaul and Testing](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/show/AC43-5/)** — Directly relevant for Lycoming engine overhaul.
- **[AC43-9 — Modifications, Repairs, and the Form CAA337](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/show/AC43-9/)** — How to document modifications and major repairs.
- **[AC43-14 — Standard Design Changes — Acceptable Technical Data](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/show/AC43-14/)** — Acceptable data sources for maintenance and modifications.
- **[AC21-2 — Airworthiness Certificates](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/rules/advisory-circulars/show/AC21-2/)** — Airworthiness certificate issuance and renewal.

### Airworthiness Directives

- **[Airworthiness Directives search](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/aircraft/airworthiness-directives/)** — Searchable database of all NZ AD schedules, filterable by category
- **[Airtourer Series AD Schedule](https://www.aviation.govt.nz/aircraft/airworthiness-directives/show/1084)** — The AD schedule specifically for the Airtourer Series (NZ Aerospace)

## United States — Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)

The [FAA](https://www.faa.gov/) is the US Government authority responsible for aviation regulation, including aircraft certification, registration, airworthiness, and maintenance standards.

### Federal Aviation Regulations (Title 14 CFR)

All regulations are published in Title 14 of the Code of Federal Regulations and available via the [Electronic Code of Federal Regulations (eCFR)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14).

#### Certification and Airworthiness Directives

- **[14 CFR Part 21 — Certification Procedures for Products and Articles](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-21)**
  Governs type certificates, supplemental type certificates, production approvals, and airworthiness certificates. Subpart H covers the issuance of Standard Airworthiness Certificates.

- **[14 CFR Part 39 — Airworthiness Directives](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-39)**
  ADs are legally enforceable rules requiring inspections, repairs, modifications, or operating limitations to address unsafe conditions. No person may operate a product to which an AD applies except in accordance with that AD (Section 39.7).

- **[14 CFR Part 23 — Airworthiness Standards: Normal Category Airplanes](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-23)**
  The airworthiness standards under which Normal category aircraft such as the Airtourer are type-certificated. Relevant when evaluating modifications, repairs, or supplemental type certificates.

#### Maintenance

- **[14 CFR Part 43 — Maintenance, Preventive Maintenance, Rebuilding, and Alteration](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-43)**
  The core maintenance regulation. Key sections include:
  - **Section 43.3** — Who may perform maintenance (certificated A&P mechanics, repair stations, and pilot/owners for preventive maintenance per Appendix A)
  - **Section 43.7** — Who may approve return to service
  - **Section 43.9** — Content, form, and disposition of maintenance records
  - **Section 43.11** — Records for inspections (annual and 100-hour)
  - **Section 43.13** — Work must use methods prescribed in the manufacturer's maintenance manual or acceptable to the Administrator
  - **Appendix A** — Defines major alterations, major repairs, and preventive maintenance an owner may perform
  - **Appendix D** — Scope and detail requirements for annual and 100-hour inspections

#### Operating Rules

- **[14 CFR Part 91 — General Operating and Flight Rules](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-F/part-91)**
  Contains the operating and maintenance obligations for aircraft owners and operators:
  - **Section 91.7** — No person may operate an aircraft unless it is in airworthy condition
  - **Section 91.203** — Aircraft must carry a valid airworthiness certificate and registration
  - **Section 91.403** — The owner or operator is primarily responsible for maintaining the aircraft in airworthy condition
  - **Section 91.409** — Requires an annual inspection within the preceding 12 calendar months (performed by an IA holder); 100-hour inspection also required if operated for hire
  - **Section 91.411** — Altimeter system and altitude reporting equipment tests every 24 calendar months (required for IFR operations)
  - **Section 91.413** — ATC transponder tests every 24 calendar months
  - **Section 91.417** — Maintenance records: owner must keep records of all maintenance, AD compliance status, and life-limited parts status; records transfer with the aircraft on sale

#### Registration and Mechanic Certification

- **[14 CFR Part 45 — Identification and Registration Marking](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-45)**
  Governs data plates and N-number registration markings.

- **[14 CFR Part 65 — Certification: Airmen Other Than Flight Crewmembers](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-D/part-65)**
  Covers A&P mechanic and Inspection Authorization (IA) certification. Defines who is authorised to perform maintenance and sign off annual inspections.

### Advisory Circulars

- **[AC 43.13-1B — Acceptable Methods, Techniques, and Practices — Aircraft Inspection and Repair](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/advisory_circulars)** — The foundational reference for aircraft structural repair and inspection methods.
- **AC 43.13-2B — Acceptable Methods, Techniques, and Practices — Aircraft Alterations** — Companion to AC 43.13-1B covering alteration methods.
- **AC 43-4A — Corrosion Control for Aircraft** — Essential for maintaining structural integrity, particularly in coastal or humid environments.
- **AC 43-12A — Preventive Maintenance** — Defines what owner/operators may perform without an A&P certificate.
- **AC 39-7D — Airworthiness Directives** — Explains the AD system, applicability, and compliance requirements.
- **AC 91-56B — Continuing Airworthiness of Older Aircraft** — Addresses practices for maintaining ageing general aviation aircraft.

Advisory Circulars can be searched at the [FAA Advisory Circulars page](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/advisory_circulars).

### Airworthiness Directives

- **[FAA AD Search](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/airworthiness_directives)** — Search by aircraft make/model, engine type, or propeller
- **[Regulatory and Guidance Library (RGL)](https://rgl.faa.gov/)** — The main FAA portal for searching ADs, Type Certificate Data Sheets, STCs, and Advisory Circulars

For an Airtourer, search for ADs under the type certificate holder name. Also search separately for engine (Lycoming) and propeller (Hartzell, Sensenich) ADs.

### Type Certificates and STCs

- **[Type Certificate Data Sheet (TCDS) Search](https://rgl.faa.gov/Regulatory_and_Guidance_Library/rgMakeModel.nsf/Frameset?OpenPage)** — Look up the approved type design including weight limits, engine options, and required equipment
- **[Supplemental Type Certificate (STC) Search](https://rgl.faa.gov/Regulatory_and_Guidance_Library/rgSTC.nsf/Frameset?OpenPage)** — Search for approved modifications
